// About

Background

I'm an Integrated Science and Technology student at James Madison University, pursuing a Bachelor of Science through the College of Integrated Science and Engineering (expected May 2028). ISAT gives me a broad technical foundation, but most of my learning happens outside the classroom — in a workshop, with a soldering iron or an oscilloscope in hand.

Recent projects span analog signal conditioning, embedded display systems, and motor control, alongside ongoing work with fabrication equipment like a plasma cutter. I care about understanding a system from first principles, whether that means debugging a saturated op-amp output or tracing an SPI bus conflict late at night.
